Introducction


The Little Prince is a work that, as the author says, is not written by an adult for children, but written by a child for adults. This book takes us through the journey of the little prince, and his characteristic way of looking at the importance of the simple and simple things in life. which reminds us how difficult it is to be a child and live in an adult world, the book encourages us to reflect on various fundamental issues in the emotional growth of a person, using metaphors and allegories.

The characters in this work are quite peculiar, and abundant, most of them only have a small participation in the life of the little prince, but they are all very important, let's see a small description of each one.


the little prince:

The main character of the work is described as a very beautiful boy with golden hair, a green suit, and a red scarf. This little boy has great curiosity about the world around him and always asks many questions. He hates adults. because he says they are always confusing everything.

the airplane pilot

The pilot is the first and last character that the little prince meets in the story, the pilot was stranded in the desert and there he met the little prince when he came to earth, the pilot represents nothing more and nothing less than the author.

The Rose

She is the little prince's dearest friend. He describes her as small and fragile, but very proud and arrogant, and although the little prince takes care of her and worries about her, she behaves arrogantly.

The population of the planets

These are short characters in the story, and each one represents a part of the character of adults, that the little prince cannot understand or dislikes, they are all represented as older men who have forgotten their inner child and live "lost in things bored with adults". these are:

The king:which represents ego, arrogance and superiority complex.

The vain:This represents vanity, superficiality and also the ego.

the drinker:It represents excesses, anxiety, fear and lack of discipline.

The businessman: It represents bitterness, overwork and the loss of the spiritual vision of life, which makes us appreciate the small things.

the lamplighter:represents the tired routine of people who try to keep up with everyone, however this seemed less ridiculous to the little prince than the avitantes of the previous planets, since at least the lamplighter was busy with "something more than himself." same."

The geographer: this one in particular represents ignorance and fear of leaving the comfort zone.

The fox

The fox is a very important character for the story, it is the fox who teaches the little prince the importance of "letting yourself be tamed" and taking care of your friendships, how when you make a friend he becomes the only one in the world for you.




the journey of the little prince

The work begins with a story about the author's childhood, when he wanted to be a cartoonist, and the adults did not understand his drawings, and instead of encouraging him to improve, they scolded him and urged him to do more serious things like study mathematics, geography and grammar. In this way he dedicated himself to studying and pleasing adults, and over time he became a pilot.

On one of his trips his plane fell in the Sahara desert, where while he was trying to repair his plane, he met a very young boy. peculiar, that he did not seem tired or bothered by the heat of the desert, who without asking anything asks him to draw a lamb, in this way the pilot begins to know the little prince better, and by the wonders he speaks he realizes that he does not He is not from this planet,

but lives on an asteroid with a rose and two volcanoes, and spends his days watching the sunsets and pulling up weeds, until one day he decides to leave to see many places and make a friend on his journey. He passes through various planets, the planet of the king where he tries to make him his servant and always orders him what to do, the planet of the businessman who only thinks about possessing everything, the planet of the vain who only saw others as admirers the planet of the drinker who drinks to forget his shame, for the planet of the lamplighter who is tired of his laborious routine, and for the planet of the geographer who claims to know many places but has not left his planet,

after telling the pilot about his trip, he also tells him about his journey in the land of the garden of roses that he found and that reminded him of his rose, and of the fox that taught him to tame a friend, and that told him a secret before saying goodbye "what is essential is invisible to the eyes, it can only be seen well with the heart" after

Throughout his trip, the little prince wants to return to his planet, he takes the lamb that the pilot drew for him, and with great regret at not seeing his friends again, the pilot and the fox return to their planet. The pilot finally repairs his plane and returns to his home, very sad and worried about his little prince. where six years later he prepares to write his story.



the writer's main argument

It is interesting to see how the author, through all the elements of the work, tries to invite us to rediscover the simple truths of life that we tend to forget when we become adults.

He shows us how when we get older we focus on the superficial and forget about feelings. profound, losing the ability to be amazed by the everyday.
He leads us to reflect on how adults are often so trapped in our small worlds that we can no longer appreciate what is "invisible to the eyes."



Personal criticism of the work

From my point of view, the little prince is a story about how a child can see the essential things in life more clearly than we adults. The little prince invites us in all its chapters to take a break, to get out of our own minds , of business, excesses, ego and vanity and sit down to think about what is really the essential thing in life, and recover our vision, learn to tame friends, and see the beauty of a flower.
